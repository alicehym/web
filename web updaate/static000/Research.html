Research

Welcome to my website. I am a PhD candidate in Political Science at Binghamton University. Prior to starting my doctoral candidacy in Binghamton University, I have a Masters Degree in International Affairs from the Pennsylvania State University and a Bachelor's Degree from SUNY Stony Brook. I study international relations and comparative politics and I am interested in political violence, protests, cybersecurity, and Non-Governmental Organizations. I am also interested in understanding the dynamics of state tolerance of protests in an authoritarian setting and the role of media in the decision-making process of such events.

From 2022-now I have been working as a Lecturer and a Teaching Assistant for Digital and Data Studies department at Binghamton University where I teach general Data Science coding courses using R and Python and Introductory Statistics classes.

I use a variety of research methodologies in my work including cross-national large N, rare events, qualitative and quantitative comparative case studies and experimental designs. I am a Political Scientist and data analyst by training but also interested in state treatment of NGOs as well as research involving the UN SDGs. My prior experience interning for NGOs and organizing youth conferences at the United Nations further facilitated my interest in these research areas.
